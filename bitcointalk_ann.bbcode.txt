[b][size=14pt]◆ ABSTRACT[/size][/b]

Cloud Service is a new Layer 1 Proof-of-Work blockchain whose founding mission is stated in its genesis block:

[quote]"01/May/2026 — 94% of Global Cloud Controlled by Big Tech as Computing Demand Soars. Now It Belongs to Everyone."[/quote]

Cloud Service is built on the Flux/Zcash protocol codebase — not a naive Bitcoin clone. It inherits Equihash-based PoW with GPU-friendly ZelHash, Sapling shielded transactions, DigiShield + LWMA difficulty adjustment, and a tiered node infrastructure layer. The network is designed to underpin a decentralized public cloud: miners secure the ledger, nodes provide compute capacity, and the protocol funds its own ecosystem without relying on an ICO, pre-sale, or external venture capital.

[list]
[li][b]Block 1:[/b] 13,020,000 CS — initial infrastructure allocation (see fund disclosure below)[/li]
[li][b]Blocks 2–10,000:[/b] Slow-start ramp (linearly increasing from ~0 to 150 CS)[/li]
[li][b]Blocks 10,001–20,000:[/b] Slow-start ramp continuation (linearly approaching 150 CS)[/li]
[li][b]Blocks 20,001–655,350:[/b] 150 CS per block[/li]
[li][b]Halving 1 (block ~675,350):[/b] 75 CS per block[/li]
[li][b]Halving 2 (block ~1,330,700):[/b] 37.5 CS per block — [b]permanently stabilized[/b][/li]
[li][b]No further halvings after round 2.[/b] Emission continues permanently at 37.5 CS/block with no end date.[/li]
[/list]

[b]Rationale for stable emission:[/b] The 150 CS block reward is split across miners and multiple tiers of infrastructure nodes. After two halvings the reward stabilizes at 37.5 CS so that node operators retain a predictable long-term incentive without the revenue cliff that indefinite halving creates. Natural deflation occurs through lost wallets and transaction fees.

[b][size=14pt]◆ WHAT IS DIFFERENT FROM BITCOIN / FLUX?[/size][/b]

[b]Why not just use Bitcoin?[/b]
Bitcoin's 10-minute blocks, SHA-256 ASIC dominance, and absence of a native node infrastructure layer make it unsuitable as the base layer for a decentralized cloud network. Cloud Service inherits the battle-tested Flux/Zcash protocol and makes targeted changes:

[list=1]
[li][b]ZelHash (Equihash 125,4):[/b] GPU-optimized PoW algorithm. Keeps mining accessible to commodity hardware and distributes hash power geographically.[/li]
[li][b]2-minute block time:[/b] Faster finality for cloud workload settlements versus Bitcoin's 10-minute and Zcash's 75-second intervals.[/li]
[li][b]Stabilized emission (no cliff after halving 2):[/b] Prevents the node-operator revenue collapse that undermines long-lived infrastructure networks.[/li]
[li][b]New genesis chain:[/b] Cloud Service is not a merge-mine or airdrop derivative. It is a standalone new chain with its own genesis block, magic bytes (0xC5 0x53), and P2P ports.[/li]
[li][b]CS-specific DNS seeds and network identity:[/b] Fully independent P2P network (seed.cscloudservice.com).[/li]
[li][b]Sapling privacy layer inherited:[/b] Shielded transactions available from genesis, providing optional privacy for compute service payments.[/li]
[li][b]Tiered node infrastructure:[/b] P2SH-secured node registration allows operators to contribute compute resources and earn a share of the block reward.[/li]
[/list]
